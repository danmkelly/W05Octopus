**Channel rationale:**
- **Touch 1 = SMS** for all cohorts: highest open rate (~98%), lands in the moment. The "saved you a spot" message mirrors the §1 verbatim exactly. SMS is the channel the member explicitly asked for.
- **Touch 2 = Email** for all cohorts: space for warmth, story, and the "why" behind the comeback. Email suits the 3-4 paragraph format that builds emotional permission to return.
- **Touch 3 = SMS for Cohorts A & C, Email for Cohort B:**
  - A & C have fewer members (43 + 27 = 70); SMS cost is negligible and these cohorts benefit from the intimacy of a text.
  - Cohort B (115 members) gets a final email to manage SMS cost within the €2,000 budget while still delivering a closing nudge.

**Budget note:** 195 SMS sends ≈ €11-15 via Twilio (Ireland rates). 195 email sends ≈ €0 (existing provider). Total campaign messaging cost under €20 — well within the €2,000 budget. The bulk of budget is reserved for the comeback page build, Instagram boosted posts, and any paid social targeting the at-risk lookalike audience.

## 5. GDPR Check

### Legal Basis

FlexHaus members consented to service communications at sign-up (§2.5: *"members consented to service comms when they joined"*). The win-back sequence falls under **legitimate interest** — it is a service message about an active membership the member is paying for, not cold marketing. Each message:

- Has a **clear, specific purpose** (inviting the member to use the class they're already paying for)
- Is **proportional** (3 touches over 7 days, then silence)
- Is **expected** (a gym communicating about class bookings to an active paying member)

### Per-Message Audit

| Message | Clear Purpose | Easy Opt-Out | No Dark Patterns |
|---------|:------------:|:------------:|:----------------:|
| A-T1 (SMS) | ✓ "Book your next class" | ✓ "Reply STOP to opt out" — standard, unambiguous, one-word | ✓ No urgency, no shame, no pre-ticked boxes |
| A-T2 (Email) | ✓ "Your second class is the one that counts" | ✓ Unsubscribe link with plain-language text | ✓ No false scarcity, no "only X spots left" |
| A-T3 (SMS) | ✓ "We saved you a spot" | ✓ "Reply STOP to opt out" | ✓ No pressure — "whenever you're ready" |
| B-T1 (SMS) | ✓ "Your spot on Tuesday is still here" | ✓ "Reply STOP to opt out" | ✓ No guilt — "it's your class if you want it" |
| B-T2 (Email) | ✓ "Your membership is still working" | ✓ Unsubscribe link, explicit separation from membership status | ✓ No forced continuity threat — "Your membership and your spot remain unchanged" |
| B-T3 (Email) | ✓ "One class. That's all it takes." | ✓ Unsubscribe link, warm language | ✓ Explicitly states "No guilt if now isn't the time" |

| C-T3 (SMS) | ✓ "Your spot, your class, your time" | ✓ "Reply STOP to opt out" | ✓ "Whenever you're ready" — member controls timing |

### Additional GDPR Safeguards

1. **No pre-ticked consent:** The comeback-page booking form captures only the booking intent (class + slot + contact) with a plain-English consent line. No pre-ticked boxes. No bundled consent for future marketing. Specified in Mateo's design brief and Kojo's build spec (§5.2.5).

2. **Opt-out does not affect membership:** Every email unsubscribe line explicitly states that opting out of win-back messages does not alter membership status or access. The member is not punished for declining comms.

3. **Data minimisation:** The personalised link carries only the `member_id` parameter — enough to pre-fill the booking form with the member's known class preferences. No demographic, behavioural, or financial data in the URL.

4. **Retention period:** The win-back sequence runs for 7 days per cohort. Members who do not engage within 30 days of Touch 1 are not re-contacted under this campaign. No indefinite drip.

5. **Right to object:** The "Reply STOP" mechanism satisfies the right to object to direct marketing under Article 21 of GDPR for SMS. The unsubscribe link satisfies it for email. Both are functional on every message in the sequence.

6. **No automated decision-making:** The cohort assignment is a segmentation rule applied by Tara's analysis, not an automated individual decision with legal effect. No member is denied service or charged differently based on their cohort.

## 6. Escalation Notes

1. **Personalisation depth:** The current copy uses `[Name]` and `[member_id]` as placeholders. Full personalisation (first name from CRM, last-booked instructor pre-filled on the page) requires the comeback page to query member data at render time. If Kojo's GitHub Pages build cannot do server-side lookup, the `member_id` URL parameter is the fallback — the page can still pre-fill the default re-entry slot (Tuesday 9am Strength with Marta) and the form captures the rest. **No escalation needed; placeholder format works for static deploy.**

2. **Instagram boosted-post budget:** The €2,000 budget covers the full campaign. If boosted-post spend needs a specific number, I'd recommend €150-300 total (€75-150 per post over 7 days, Dublin + 25km radius, ages 25-45, fitness interests). This is within budget but not specified in the brief — flagged as `[ASSUMPTION]`.

3. **No invented stats confirmed:** The only CSV-derived number used in copy is the 1.98× churn lift ("nearly twice as likely"), and it appears only in the Cohort A email where it's directly relevant. No other numbers are claimed. No testimonials are fabricated. No class-capacity figures are invented.
