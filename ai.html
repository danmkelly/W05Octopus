What is AI?

AI (artificial intelligence) is software that can do things we used to think only humans could do — like writing, drawing, answering questions, or spotting patterns in a spreadsheet. You type or speak to it in plain English, and it responds like a helpful assistant.

Think of AI like a part-time staff member who never sleeps, reads everything you give them instantly, and can write your social media posts, answer customer emails, or suggest a new weekly special based on what sold well last month. It makes mistakes, just like a new hire would — so you still check its work.

🤝 What About AI Agents?

You might have heard the term "AI agent" — it's worth knowing what it means because it comes up a lot. A regular AI (like ChatGPT) is a chat — you ask, it answers, you ask again. An AI agent is different: you give it a goal, and it works through a series of steps on its own to get the job done. It's the difference between asking someone for directions and handing them the car keys.

Basic AI Chat
Ask a question → get an answer. You ask again → it responds again. You drive the conversation.
AI Agent
Give it a goal → it plans the steps → it takes actions (browses, fills forms, sends emails) → it comes back to you with the result.
Real Example
"Research the best wholesalers for organic flour in Dublin, compare their prices, and draft an enquiry email to the top three." The agent researches, compares, and drafts — while you do something else.

Right now, agent features are still emerging. ChatGPT's "Tasks" can schedule reminders and run prompts for you. Claude can use tools when set up via an API (a bit more technical). For most small business owners, the key thing is: know agents exist, understand the difference from a simple chat, and keep an eye on them — they'll likely become a bigger part of these tools over the next year or two.

Bottom line: a chatbot is a conversation. An agent is a worker. You're still the boss — the agent just gets on with the job.

Why AI Matters for Your Small Business

✍️

Write your content faster

AI can draft Instagram captions, Facebook posts, email newsletters, website text, and product descriptions in seconds. A dog walker in Ranelagh can produce a week of social posts in 15 minutes instead of two hours.

💬

Answer routine questions 24/7

AI chatbots can handle "What are your opening hours?" or "Do you deliver to Dublin 8?" while you're asleep or serving customers. Handy out for hospitality and retail where people message outside business hours.

📊

Make sense of your own data

Paste a spreadsheet of last month's sales and ask: "Which products sold best on Saturdays?" No accountant or Excel wizard needed — just a bit of plain-English asking.

🧭

Brainstorm and problem-solve

Stuck for a name for your new pastry? Need a pricing idea for a spring promotion? AI works as a fast, free thinking partner. (Always double-check regulatory and legal answers on official Irish sites.)

On this course you're building a business plan — here's a bit of help with the bits and pieces that come after. AI won't build your business for you, but it can take a load of the busy-work off your plate so you can focus on serving your community, chatting to customers, and making the big decisions yourself.

The person down the road running a high-street shop already has enough on — ordering stock, managing staff rotas, doing the books, and keeping the social media ticking over. That's where AI earns its keep: doing the first-draft work so you can spend more time on the parts of the business only you can do.

Practical Use Cases

Here's how real small businesses around Dublin are putting AI to work — organised by the bits of running a business that actually matter.

Marketing

Scenario

A café owner needs fresh Instagram captions for a new drink launch.

What you'd do

Ask ChatGPT: "Write 5 Instagram captions for a Dublin café launching a new oat-milk flat white. Keep it warm and friendly, mention it's Irish oat milk, include emojis, and end with a call to visit."

Scenario

A freelance designer needs a case study and cover image for their portfolio.

What you'd do

Use Canva AI's Magic Write to draft the case study. Generate a hero image with DALL·E for the cover. Whole thing: about 20 minutes.

Scenario

A retail shop wants to tie a window display to upcoming local events.

What you'd do

Ask Perplexity: "What events are happening in Dublin city centre next month that I could tie a shop window display to?"

Customer Service

Scenario

A dog walker needs an FAQ page for their website but doesn't know where to start.

What you'd do

Ask ChatGPT: "Write 10 FAQ questions and answers for a Dublin dog-walking business. Cover pricing, areas served, bad weather, insurance, how keys are handled."

Scenario

A café got a negative Google review and needs to reply thoughtfully.

What you'd do

Ask ChatGPT: "A customer left a 2-star review saying our soup was cold and service was slow last Saturday. Write a short, genuine reply that apologises, explains it was unusually busy due to a match at Croke Park, and invites them back for a free coffee."

Scenario

A plumber wants to turn a common repair tip into a helpful blog post.

What you'd do

Record a voice note in Otter.ai explaining "why your radiator is cold at the top." Get the transcript, tidy it up with ChatGPT, and post it as a blog post. There's no bother.

Operations

Scenario

A retail shop wants to know which products are making money and which aren't.

What you'd do

Paste your inventory spreadsheet into ChatGPT: "Which 5 products have the highest profit margin? Which 3 are selling slowest?"

Scenario

A café owner needs a weekly staff rota.

What you'd do

Ask AI: "Create a weekly staff rota table for a café open Mon–Sat 7am–4pm with 2 baristas and 1 floor person per shift. Include a column for total hours."

Scenario

A service business wants a consistent morning opening routine.

What you'd do

Ask ChatGPT: "Write a checklist for opening a small retail shop in Dublin each morning. Include till setup, cleaning, stock check, and a quick team huddle."

Finance & Admin

Scenario

A sole trader wants to know what home-office expenses they can claim.

What you'd do

Ask ChatGPT: "I'm a sole trader in Ireland. What expenses can I claim against tax for a home office? List them in plain English." Then confirm the details with your accountant.

Scenario

A freelance designer wants to make their invoice emails sound more professional.

What you'd do

Paste your draft into ChatGPT: "Make this sound more professional but still friendly. Add a clear payment due date and bank details placeholder."

Scenario

A café owner needs a simple cash-flow tracker — especially heading into Christmas trade rush.

What you'd do

Ask AI: "Write a simple weekly cash-flow tracker template for a small Dublin café. Columns for: day, till take, card take, total revenue, stock delivered cost, wages, rent, miscellaneous, and net."

Limitations and Caveats

🟠

Worth knowing before you dive in

  • 1. AI makes things up (hallucination). AI sometimes invents facts, names, dates, and numbers that sound correct but are completely false. Treat everything as a first draft or suggestion — never as verified fact. Always double-check anything that matters legally, financially, or for customer safety.
  • 2. Don't paste confidential information. Anything you type into a free AI tool may be used to train the provider's models. Never paste client names, employee data, bank details, or confidential financials. If you wouldn't post it on a public noticeboard on Grafton Street, don't put it into a free AI tool.
  • 3. AI is a tool, not a replacement for human judgment. AI can draft a contract clause but it cannot be your solicitor. It can suggest a tax deduction but it is not your accountant. Use AI to save time — never to replace your own thinking on important decisions.
  • 4. Cost considerations. Most tools have generous free tiers. Paid plans typically €18–€25/month. Start free. Only pay when you hit a real limitation. By then you'll know exactly what you need.
  • 5. Irish-specific context gaps. Most AI tools are trained primarily on US and UK data. They may not know Irish-specific regulations in detail. Use AI for direction and ideas, but confirm details on official Irish government websites (gov.ie, revenue.ie, citizensinformation.ie).

How to Get Started

1

Create a free account

Go to chatgpt.com. Click "Sign up." Use your email or Google account. No credit card needed. That's you in — there's no bother.

2

Ask your first question

Type something real to your business. Example: "Write a short, friendly welcome message for the homepage of a Dublin dog-grooming business called Happy Paws."

3

Refine your question (this is called "prompting")

Ask a follow-up: "Make it shorter." Or "Make it more casual, like I'm talking to a neighbour." Or "Add a mention that we serve Clontarf, Raheny, and Killester." This skill — asking, refining, asking again — is 80% of using AI well.

4

Ask something practical for your business this week

Pick one task from the Use Cases section above — a social media caption, an FAQ page, a customer email reply, a rota template. Ask AI to draft it. You'll have a first draft in under 2 minutes. That's grand, isn't it?

5

Try one image tool

Go to copilot.microsoft.com (free). Type: "Create an image of a cosy Dublin café interior with exposed brick, warm lighting, and customers reading newspapers." You now have a free image for social media or a flyer — handy out for the Christmas trade rush.

AI for Navigating Business Supports in South Dublin

One of the trickiest bits of starting a business is figuring out what support is out there — grants, mentoring, training, tax guidance, legal registration. There's plenty of help available in South Dublin, but finding it and understanding what applies to you takes time. AI can speed up that research enormously.

🔍

Find grants and funding

Ask AI: "What grants are available for a new café in South Dublin in 2025? Include Local Enterprise Office, Enterprise Ireland, and council-level supports." AI will give you a starting list — then verify on the LEO site.

📝

Understand the registration process

Ask AI: "Walk me through registering as a sole trader in Ireland step by step. What forms do I need? What deadlines apply?" Use the answer as your checklist, and confirm on revenue.ie.

📋

Translate official language

Paste a chunk of text from a government website and ask: "Can you explain this in plain English?" AI cuts through the jargon so you actually understand what's required.

📌

Key organisations to know (and verify with)

  • Local Enterprise Office South Dublin — Your first port of call. Free mentoring, start-your-own-business courses, feasibility grants, and the Trading Online Voucher. Based in Tallaght. Website: localenterprise.ie/SouthDublin
  • Enterprise Ireland — Supports for businesses with export potential. If you're planning to sell beyond Ireland (even online), they're worth a look. Website: enterprise-ireland.com
  • Revenue.ie — Tax registration, VAT, and everything Revenue-related. AI can summarise guidance but never treat its answers as formal tax advice. Always confirm on the official site.
  • Companies Registration Office (CRO) — If you're forming a limited company rather than trading as a sole trader. Website: cro.ie
  • South Dublin County Council — Business rates, planning permission, street trading licences, and local economic development initiatives. Their website and customer service team are the definitive source.

A handy prompt to get you started: "I'm starting a [type of business] in South Dublin. What supports, grants, and training are available to me? Include Local Enterprise Office, Enterprise Ireland, and council-level programmes. Tell me what I can apply for, what the eligibility criteria are, and where to find the official forms."

Glossary

AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Software that can perform tasks that normally need human thinking, such as writing, drawing, or answering questions.

Prompt

The instruction or question you type into an AI tool — essentially, "what you ask it to do."

Agent (AI Agent)

An AI that can take actions and complete multi-step tasks on its own — not just answer questions. Imagine giving it a goal instead of a single question, and having it work through the steps while you get on with other things.

LLM (Large Language Model)

The engine under the hood of tools like ChatGPT and Claude; a giant pattern-matching system trained on a huge amount of text from the internet.

Generative AI

AI that creates new content (text, images, audio, code) rather than just analysing existing data. ChatGPT and DALL·E are generative AI.

Hallucination

When AI confidently produces information that sounds correct but is completely made up — like a very convincing liar.

Training Data

The massive collection of text, images, and other information the AI was "taught" on before you ever used it. Think of it as the AI's textbooks.

Token

A chunk of text — roughly three-quarters of a word — that AI processes. Most free tools let you process thousands of tokens per conversation.

API (Application Programming Interface)

A way for one piece of software to talk to another behind the scenes. You won't use this directly as a beginner, but you may see it mentioned.

Fine-tuning

Training an existing AI model on a specific, smaller set of data (e.g. your product catalogue) so it becomes better at your particular task. Usually not needed for small businesses.

Chatbot

A piece of software you can have a typed conversation with. ChatGPT is a chatbot. The AI customer-service tools on websites are also chatbots.
