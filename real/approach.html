The Pipeline

The Real Brief received the full harness — a 298-line context file with backstory, data summaries, voice-of-member verbatims, brand direction, named specialist contracts, a mandatory three.js spec, and a Cold Verify STOP gate. This pipeline included a blind audit step absent from the Control version.

Yellow (Tara) ─▶ Red-Orange (Mateo) ∥ Green (Róisín) ─▶ Blue (Kojo) ─▶ Yellow (Cold Verify) ─▶ Purple (STOP gate) Diagnosis Design spec + Win-back copy three.js page Blind audit Ship / fix

Agent-by-Agent Breakdown

Stage 1 — Intelligence

🟡 Yellow: Tara (Membership Retention & Churn Analyst)

Rebuilt the entire member panel from the 45,349-row CSV (1,556 members). Ran five prescribed analyses: (1) member panel with tenure and attendance breakdowns, (2) churn curve proving 1.98× lift for one-and-done members (75.3% vs 38.1%), (3) at-risk segment defined precisely: active AND (total_bookings ≤ 2 OR days_since_last_booking ≥ 30) = 195 members / €8,797/mo / €105,564/year, (4) no-show patterns by segment, and (5) reactivation lever identification (Marta at 64.4% return rate). Named the ONE metric: comeback-class bookings within 30 days. Flagged three data anomalies (see below).

Artifacts: diagnosis.md (465 lines) · member_panel.csv (1,556 rows)
Stage 2a — Design

🟠 Red-Orange: Mateo (Conversion-Led 3D Experience Designer)

Delivered a 1,198-line design spec for the three.js comeback-booking page: four-stage scroll choreography (resting pulse → gym fly-through → proof columns → Volt-green card pool), brand palette (Carbon/Chalk/Volt/Ember), typography system (Anton/Inter), component specs for all sections, five interaction states, reduced-motion and no-WebGL fallback designs, comprehensive accessibility plan, and five performance trade-off flags. Every design decision traced to Tara's diagnosis.

Artifacts: design-spec.md (1,198 lines)
Stage 2b — Distribution

🟢 Green: Róisín (Fitness Lifecycle & Win-Back Copywriter)

Wrote a complete 3-touch win-back sequence across three behavioural cohorts: (A) Fresh one-and-done (43 members), (B) Long-lapsed guilt-payers (115 members), (C) Quietly stopped regulars (27 members). Each cohort gets SMS → Email → Final Nudge with cohort-specific messaging spines. Included two Instagram posts, three hero copy variants for the comeback page, a 15-row research-to-copy traceability table, and a per-message GDPR audit. Zero invented statistics.

Artifacts: marketing.md (416 lines)
Stage 3 — Build

🔵 Blue: Kojo (three.js + Static-Deploy Front-End Engineer)

Built a 1,881-line single-file three.js comeback-booking page. Procedural 3D scenes: pulse line (TubeGeometry), gym fly-through (InstancedMesh rig pillars, floor tiles, wall panels), chalk-dust GPU particles (Points + AdditiveBlending), proof columns with scroll-staggered reveals, Volt-green light pool (semi-transparent planes). Device tier detection (high/mid/low), DPR capped at 2, delta-time lerp damping, no post-processing bloom (CSS box-shadow glow instead). Complete form with Formspree/mailto fallback. Reduced-motion and no-WebGL fallbacks.

Artifacts: real/index.html (1,881 lines, 65 KB)
Stage 4 — Audit

🟡 Yellow: Cold Verifier (Blind, Independent)

Audited all five deliverables against six frozen criteria from the harness. Scored 6/6 PASS on: data-bound diagnosis, one-metric focus, on-brand voice, GDPR compliance, and no hallucinated facts. The page scored PASS-with-gaps (IntersectionObserver omission, proof beat stagger unimplemented). Found one critical catch: the URL parameter contract between Róisín and Kojo was broken — marketing links passed ?id= and ?slot= but the page only read ?cohort=. This severed the personalisation chain and campaign measurement loop. The Purple Manager fixed it before shipping.

Artifacts: cold-verify.md (156 lines)

The Catch: What The Cold Verifier Found

⚠ BROKEN HANDOFF: URL Parameter Contract

Róisín's marketing links used ?id=[member_id]&slot=tue-9am-strength but Kojo's page read only ?cohort=a|b|c. The personalisation chain — member-specific pre-fill, slot pre-selection, conversion tracking — was severed. When a member tapped a win-back link, the page could not pre-fill their name, known class preferences, or track which at-risk member converted.

Resolution: The Purple Manager added id, name, and slot parameter parsing with dynamic form pre-fill and a hidden member_id field before shipping.

Non-blocking gaps (not fixed before shipping): No IntersectionObserver for DOM crossfades (JS-driven opacity in rAF instead), proof beat stagger unimplemented (all three fade as one unit), and the marketing copy's three cohort counts (43+115+27=185) don't sum to the 195 total — the 10 "2 bookings, recent" borderline members are unaccounted for in the copy segmentation.

Data Anomalies Tara Found

⚠ MRR Mismatch: Computed active MRR = €28,645 (637 members × €44.97 avg) vs the §2.1 benchmark of ~€30,800. Likely because the benchmark includes frozen members' fees or was rounded from an earlier reference date.
⚠ Inverted Booking Buckets: The 2–4 booking bucket is 8.9% in the data vs ~27% in the summary table. The 20+ bucket is 28.8% vs ~8%. These two buckets are essentially swapped vs the harness benchmarks. The 1-booking and 5–20 buckets match perfectly. The churn lift calculation is unaffected.
⚠ Zero No-Shows for One-and-Done Members: All 655 single-booking members "attended" their only class — zero no-shows. In real-world gym data, some first-class no-shows would be expected. This is a synthetic data artifact.

What the harness bought: The rich brief gave every agent a named identity, a specific contract, brand guardrails, and a data-driven handoff chain. The 3D comeback experience, the cohort-specific win-back copy, the GDPR audit, and the blind quality gate are all products of the context — not a smarter model. The page doesn't just book a class; it tells the member they were remembered.
What the harness cost: Complexity. The three.js spec demanded a specialist builder, the Cold Verify step added a turn-around cycle, and the URL parameter mismatch required a manual fix. A simple prompt would have shipped a flat form in one pass — but it would have been a flat form.
