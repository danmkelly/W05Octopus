Control vs Real Brief: What Changed?

The model didn't get smarter. The context did. Here's what the rich harness bought — and what it cost.

Prompt 10-line raw brief 298-line harness with backstory, data summaries, voice-of-member verbatims, brand direction, named specialist contracts, mandatory three.js spec, and Cold Verify gate
Agent identity Generic roles ("Researcher", "Designer") Named specialists with domain expertise and personal contracts (Tara, Mateo, Kojo, Róisín)
Output Flat HTML booking page (53.8 KB). Functional, clean, but emotionally generic. Scroll-driven three.js comeback experience (65 KB). Branded palette, procedural 3D, device-tier adaptation, fallbacks, plus a 3-touch win-back campaign with 2 Instagram posts.
Data rigour Good. CSV queried, churn diagnosed, at-risk segment found. But no independent audit. Better. Full member panel rebuilt (1,556 rows). Three data anomalies flagged transparently. One-metric focus enforced. Every number traceable to CSV.
Brand discipline Adequate. Uses the palette and fonts from a one-line brief. No brand voice guidance. Strong. Carbon/Chalk/Volt/Ember palette, Anton/Inter type, "Comeback / we saved you a spot" spine, warm-not-shaming tone. Unmistakably FlexHaus.
Quality gate None. Manager reviewed but no blind audit. Errors could slip through. Cold Verify. Independent blind auditor scored 6/6 criteria, caught a broken URL handoff, and named non-blocking gaps. Fixed before shipping.
Strategic depth Page-centric. A booking form with good copy. No follow-up campaign, no channel strategy, no cohort segmentation. Campaign-wide. Three behavioural cohorts with distinct messaging spines, SMS/Email/Instagram channel mix, GDPR-audited, metric-traceable. The page is the centrepiece, not the whole plan.
Performance Fast. 53.8 KB, zero JavaScript overhead, loads instantly. Works on any device. Managed. 65 KB + three.js CDN payload (~150 KB gzipped). Device-tier detection, DPR cap, procedural geometry. Mobile-first. Fallback page works with zero 3D.
Risk of hallucination Low. Agents derived everything from the CSV. No pre-digested summaries to trust blindly. Contained. The harness's summary tables had errors (inverted booking buckets, MRR mismatch) but Tara caught them by computing fresh from CSV. The Cold Verifier caught the remaining handoff break.
